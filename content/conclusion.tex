\section{Conclusion}

We investigated the fundamental drivers of thermodynamic fidelity and zero-shot transferability in machine learning force fields, demonstrating that chemical context-aware embeddings and training data composition dictate dynamic sampling quality far more than raw architectural scale. On the peptide systems investigated, utilizing sequence-aware topological representations achieved superior results across dynamic metrics, notably surpassing a rigorous generalized Born implicit solvent baseline on an entirely unseen molecular graph. Furthermore, our trajectory length ablations revealed that highly truncated, 5-nanosecond simulations contain sufficient thermodynamic information to accurately learn the underlying Boltzmann distribution, offering massive reductions in the computational cost required for training data generation. 

Similar to how explicit thermodynamic integration is an essential tool for evaluating classical free energies, we are confident that prioritizing sequence-aware representations and explicit dynamic evaluation over static force metrics is a critical paradigm shift that will move the field of transferable machine learning force fields forward.
