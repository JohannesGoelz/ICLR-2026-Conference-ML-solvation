\begin{abstract}
Accurate treatment of solvent effects in molecular dynamics is essential but computationally expensive with explicit water. Classical implicit models, like Generalized Born (GB), reduce costs but often sacrifice thermodynamic accuracy. Here, we introduce a graph neural network trained via variational force matching to learn transferable implicit solvation potentials from explicit-solvent data. Evaluating embedding strategies against a GB baseline, we show that elemental types and continuous partial charges struggle to generalize out-of-distribution. In contrast, topological and sequence-aware representations consistently surpass the GB baseline across all in- and out-of-distribution metrics, demonstrating robust transferability. Furthermore, we demonstrate that models trained on trajectories as short as 5 ns achieve thermodynamic fidelity on par with those utilizing 500 ns simulations, reducing data generation costs by two orders of magnitude. Supported by extensive datasets (400 dipeptides, 1530 tetrapeptides) and a parallel framework for simulating ML potentials, this work establishes rigorous design principles for extending implicit solvation to diverse peptide sequences.

\end{abstract}
